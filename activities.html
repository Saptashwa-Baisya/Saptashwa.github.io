Research and Applications

This page contains reports of some of the works that I have done.

1. SRFP 2024: I spent the summer of 2024 at IISER Pune, studying under the guidance of Dr. Anup Biswas. The topic of my study was the formalization of lower dimensional objects in higher dimensional spaces. For instance, we know a triangle in our 3 dimensional space has lebesgue measure (Volume) 0, however we have a notion of it's area, which measures it 'size' and let's us compare any 2 triangles.

So, there seems to be a measure, which is not the Lebesgue measure, that measures lower dimensional objects in a higher dimensional spaces and assigns appropriate dimensions to them. This is called the Hausdorff Measure, and the dimension it assigns is called the Hausdorff Dimension. The Hausdorff Measure captures, in some sense, sets which are invisible to the Lebesgue Measure, and thus it can be used to peek at properties of functions which are restricted to very small sets.

Now, think of a Brownian Path as the path traced out by a pollen grain in water. Its highly irregular nature made it a perfect example to apply the techniques that we learnt. Hence as an application, we looked at the Hausdorff Dimension of Brownian Paths.
